The Amazon rainforest is the world's largest tropical rainforest, located in South America and spanning nine countries, with the majority in Brazil.
It is renowned for its incredible biodiversity, hosting one in ten known species on Earth, including millions of insect, plant, and animal species, some of which remain undiscovered.
The region is a complex, interconnected system of water and plant life, significantly influencing regional and global climate through its role in carbon storage and the water cycle, but it is also threatened by deforestation and climatic changes.

The Thar Desert, also known as the Great Indian Desert, is a vast arid region in the northwestern part of the Indian subcontinent, primarily in India and extending into Pakistan.
It is characterized by its sandy terrain, sparse vegetation, and extreme temperatures, with scorching hot summers and chilly winters.
The desert is home to a variety of wildlife adapted to its harsh conditions, including species like the Indian gazelle (chinkara), desert fox, and various reptiles.
Despite its challenging environment, the Thar Desert supports human settlements that have developed unique cultural practices and livelihoods, such as traditional crafts and camel herding.
